# Chapter 1: Introduction to the Real Estate Platform

## 1.1 Purpose of the Platform

Welcome to the Real Estate Platform! Our primary goal is to provide a comprehensive and user-friendly online environment for all your property-related needs. Whether you are looking to buy, sell, or rent properties, or manage your existing property portfolio, our platform is designed to make the process smoother, more transparent, and efficient.

We aim to connect:
*   **Property Seekers** with a wide range of verified listings.
*   **Property Owners/Landlords** with tools to effectively list, manage, and find tenants or buyers for their properties.
*   **Tenants** with a clear way to manage their rental agreements, payments, and communication with landlords.

Our platform strives to be your trusted partner in navigating the real estate market, offering features that simplify search, communication, and management.

## 1.2 Overview of Key Features for Users

The Real Estate Platform offers a variety of features tailored to your needs:

*   **Property Search & Discovery:**
    *   Intuitive search functionality on the homepage to quickly find properties based on type, location, and buy/rent preferences.
    *   A detailed **Property Catalogue** with advanced filtering options (price, area, bedrooms, property type, etc.) to narrow down your search.
    *   Interactive map view to explore properties geographically.
    *   Comprehensive **Property Details Pages** with image galleries, key features, specifications, descriptions, and location maps.

*   **User Interaction & Management:**
    *   **Wishlist:** Save properties you are interested in for easy access and to track their status.
    *   **Visit Booking System:** Request and manage property visits. This includes a system for purchasing visit credits through various plans.
    *   **Rental Applications:** Apply for rental properties directly through the platform after a visit.

*   **For Property Owners/Landlords:**
    *   **Property Submission:** A detailed form to list your property for sale or rent, including all necessary details, features, and images.
    *   **My Listed Properties:** A dashboard to view and manage all the properties you have listed, including their status, tenant information (if applicable), and access to edit details or manage images.
    *   **Rent & Payment Tracking (for Landlords with Managed Properties):** View outstanding rent dues and payment history for your rental properties.
    *   **Support Ticket Management (for Landlords):** View and respond to support tickets raised by tenants regarding your properties.

*   **For Tenants:**
    *   **My Rentals:** View details of properties you are currently renting through the platform.
    *   **Rent Dues:** Track upcoming and overdue rent payments.
    *   **Support Ticket Management (for Tenants):** Raise and track support tickets for issues or inquiries related to your rented property.

*   **Account Management:**
    *   **Profile Page:** Manage your basic account information and view your visit credit balance.
    *   **My Transactions:** View a history of your purchases (e.g., visit plans).
    *   **My Rental Applications:** Track the status of your submitted rental applications.

This manual will guide you through each of these features in detail.

# Chapter 2: Getting Started

This chapter will walk you through the initial steps to begin using the Real Estate Platform, from creating your account to understanding the basic navigation.

## 2.1 Creating Your Account & Logging In

To access most features of the Real Estate Platform, you'll need to create an account and log in.

**Steps to Log In/Sign Up:**

1.  Navigate to the platform's homepage.
2.  Click on the **"Login"** button, typically found in the top right corner of the navigation bar.
3.  You will be redirected to the Login page.
4.  Currently, the platform supports signing in using your **Google account**. Click on the "Sign in with Google" button.
5.  A Google authentication pop-up will appear. Select the Google account you wish to use or sign in to your Google account if you aren't already.
6.  Grant the necessary permissions if prompted.
7.  Upon successful authentication, you will be redirected back to the platform, usually to the homepage or your profile.

If this is your first time signing in with a particular Google account, a new user profile will be automatically created for you on the Real Estate Platform.

## 2.2 Verifying Your Phone Number

For security and to enable full platform functionality (like receiving important notifications or OTPs for certain actions), verifying your phone number is a crucial step after your initial login.

**How to Verify Your Phone Number:**

1.  After your first successful login, if your phone number is not yet verified, you may be automatically redirected to the "Verify Phone" page. If not, you might be prompted or can navigate there if certain actions require it.
2.  On the "Verify Phone" page:
    *   **Enter Your Mobile Number:** Input your 10-digit Indian mobile number. The country code `+91` is usually pre-filled or automatically added.
    *   **Send OTP:** Click the "Send OTP" button. An OTP (One-Time Password) will be sent to the mobile number you provided via SMS.
3.  **Enter OTP:**
    *   Once you receive the OTP, enter the 6-digit code into the OTP input field.
    *   Click the "Verify OTP" button.
4.  **Verification Status:**
    *   **Successful:** If the OTP is correct and valid, your phone number will be verified. You will typically be redirected to the page you were trying to access or back to the homepage.
    *   **Failed:** If the OTP is incorrect, expired, or there's an issue, an error message will be displayed.
        *   You can request to **"Resend OTP"** if you didn't receive it or if it expired. A timer may indicate when you can request a resend.
        *   You can also choose to **"Change phone number?"** if you entered it incorrectly.

**Important Notes:**
*   Ensure you have good network reception to receive the OTP SMS.
*   OTPs are time-sensitive and typically expire after a few minutes.
*   Do not share your OTP with anyone.

## 2.3 Navigating the Platform

Once logged in, you'll interact with the platform primarily through the Navigation Bar and the Footer.

### 2.3.1 The Navigation Bar

The navigation bar (Navbar) is located at the top of every page and provides access to the main sections of the platform.

*   **Logo:** Clicking the platform logo usually takes you back to the Homepage.
*   **Main Links:**
    *   **Catalogue:** Takes you to the main property listing page where you can browse all available properties.
    *   **Buy/Rent Dropdowns:** These provide quick links to filtered property catalogue views for specific property types (Houses, Land, Buildings, Apartments) under "Buy" (Sale) or "Rent" (Rental) categories.
    *   **Buy Visit Credits:** Navigates to the page where you can purchase visit plans.
*   **Action Buttons & User Menu (Right Side):**
    *   **Post Property:** Allows property owners to start the process of listing a new property. If not logged in, this will redirect to the Login page.
    *   **Wishlist Icon (<kbd>❤️</kbd>):** Visible when logged in. Takes you to your Wishlist page. A badge may indicate the number of items in your wishlist.
    *   **Visit Credits Display:** Shows your current visit balance and its expiry date.
    *   **User Profile Icon/Login Button:**
        *   If you are **not logged in**, this will be a "Login" button.
        *   If you are **logged in**, this will be a user icon (often your initial or a profile picture). Clicking it opens a dropdown menu with links to:
            *   My Properties (for owners)
            *   Wishlist
            *   My Rentals (for tenants)
            *   My Applications
            *   My Transactions
            *   Profile Settings
            *   Sign out
*   **Mobile Menu (<kbd>☰</kbd>):** On smaller screens, main links and user menu options are consolidated into a mobile menu, accessible by tapping the hamburger icon.

### 2.3.2 The Footer

The footer is located at the bottom of every page and contains useful links and information.

*   **Brand Information:** A brief description of the Real Estate Platform.
*   **Social Media Links:** Links to the platform's social media profiles (e.g., Facebook, X, Instagram, LinkedIn).
*   **Quick Links:** Links to main sections like Home, Properties, Visit Plans, Post Property, and About Us.
*   **Property Types Links:** Quick links to specific property type searches in the catalogue.
*   **Contact Info:** The platform's contact address, phone number, and email.
*   **Legal Links:**
    *   **Terms of Service:** Leads to the platform's terms and conditions page.
    *   **Privacy Policy:** Leads to the platform's privacy policy page.
*   **Copyright Information:** Displays the copyright year and platform name.

# Chapter 3: Finding Your Ideal Property

This chapter explains how to search, browse, and filter properties on the Real Estate Platform to find the one that best suits your needs.

## 3.1 Searching for Properties (Home Page)

The homepage provides a quick and easy way to start your property search.

1.  **Buy/Rent Tabs:**
    *   Select either **"Buy"** or **"Rent"** to specify if you are looking to purchase or lease a property. This will pre-filter your search results.
2.  **Property Type Buttons:**
    *   Click on common property type buttons like "House / Villa," "Apartment," "Land," or "Building / Commercial."
    *   Selecting a type will highlight it and add it to your search criteria. Clicking it again will deselect it.
    *   These buttons might represent a main property type (e.g., `HOUSE`) and a subtype (e.g., `APARTMENT_FLAT`).
3.  **Location Input:**
    *   Enter a location (e.g., city, locality, area name) in the search bar.
    *   The platform may attempt to auto-detect your current city if you grant location permissions. You can override this by typing your desired location.
4.  **BHK Selector (for Houses/Apartments):**
    *   If you've selected "House / Villa" or "Apartment," a BHK (Bedrooms, Hall, Kitchen) selector may appear.
    *   You can click buttons like "1 BHK," "2 BHK," "3+ BHK" to filter by the number of bedrooms. Clicking a selected BHK option again will deselect it.
5.  **Search Button:**
    *   Once you've set your criteria, click the **"Search"** button.
    *   You will be taken to the Property Catalogue page, pre-filtered with your selections.

## 3.2 Browsing the Property Catalogue

The Property Catalogue page (`/catalogue`) is where you can see all available listings and refine your search using various filters.

### 3.2.1 Using Filters

A dedicated filter sidebar (or a mobile filter drawer) allows you to apply detailed criteria to your search.

*   **Accessing Filters:**
    *   **Desktop:** The filter sidebar is usually visible on the left side of the catalogue page.
    *   **Mobile:** Tap the "Filters" button to open the filter drawer.
*   **Common Filter Options:**
    *   **Property Type:** Select "House," "Land," or "Building."
    *   **Looking For:** Choose "Buy" (Sale) or "Rent" (Rental).
    *   **Location:**
        *   **Locality Search:** Enter specific areas, neighborhoods, or street names.
        *   **City:** Specify the city.
    *   **Budget:** Set a minimum and/or maximum price range.
    *   **Area:** Define a minimum and/or maximum property area, and select the unit (e.g., sq.ft, cents, acres).
    *   **House-Specific Filters (if "House" is selected):**
        *   **Type of House:** E.g., Apartment/Flat, Independent House/Villa.
        *   **No. of Bedrooms:** Select 1, 2, 3, 4, 5+ BHK.
        *   **Furnishing Status:** E.g., Unfurnished, Semi-Furnished, Fully-Furnished.
        *   **Facing Direction:** E.g., North, South, East, West.
    *   **Land-Specific Filters (if "Land" is selected):**
        *   **Type of Land:** E.g., Residential, Commercial, Agricultural.
    *   **Building-Specific Filters (if "Building" is selected):**
        *   **Type of Building:** E.g., Office Space, Retail Shop, Warehouse.
*   **Applying Filters:** Changes to filters are typically applied automatically, or you might need to click an "Apply Filters" button in the mobile drawer.
*   **Resetting Filters:** A "Reset All" button is usually available to clear all applied filters and return to the default view.

### 3.2.2 List View vs. Map View

The Property Catalogue offers different ways to view search results:

*   **List View / Grid View:**
    *   Properties are displayed as individual cards, either in a single column (list) or multiple columns (grid, typically on wider screens).
    *   Each card provides a summary of the property.
    *   You can toggle between these views if the option is available.
*   **Map View:**
    *   Click the "Show Map" button (<kbd>🗺️</kbd>) to display properties on an interactive map.
    *   Properties with valid coordinates will appear as markers.
    *   Clicking a marker usually shows a pop-up with basic property details and a link to the full details page.
    *   The map will automatically adjust its bounds to fit the displayed properties.
    *   You can toggle this view on or off.

*   **Sorting:**
    *   A dropdown menu allows you to sort the property results by criteria like:
        *   Newest (default)
        *   Price (Low-High or High-Low)
        *   Area (Largest)

## 3.3 Understanding Property Cards

Property cards provide a quick overview of each listing in the catalogue or on the homepage.

*   **Image:** A primary photo of the property.
*   **Price:** The asking price (for sale) or monthly rent.
*   **Type & Listing Badges:** Indicates if it's a "House," "Land," or "Building," and whether it's "For Sale" or "For Rent."
*   **Featured Badge:** Highlights properties that are specially featured.
*   **Property Name/Title:** The name or a brief title for the property.
*   **Location:** Locality and city.
*   **Area:** Total area of the property (e.g., "1200 sq.ft").
*   **Key Features (Simple Card Variant):** May show 1-2 key features like number of bedrooms or type of land.
*   **Key Features (Detailed Card Variant):**
    *   **Bedrooms & Bathrooms (for Houses):** Number of beds and baths.
    *   **Parking, Furnishing, Facing (for Houses):** If applicable.
    *   **Land Type, Dimensions, Road Access (for Land):** If applicable.
    *   **Building Type, Floors, Units (for Buildings):** If applicable.
*   **Action Buttons (Detailed Card Variant):**
    *   **Book Visit/Buy Visits:** Allows you to request a site visit or prompts to buy visit credits if you have none.
    *   **Apply to Rent:** Appears for rental properties after a visit is completed.
    *   **View Application Status:** Appears if you have an active application for that rental property.
    *   **View Details:** Takes you to the full property details page.
    *   **Share Icon:** Opens a modal to share the property link.
    *   **Wishlist Icon (<kbd>❤️</kbd>):** Allows you to add or remove the property from your wishlist.

Clicking anywhere on a property card (except specific action buttons like "Book Visit" or "Wishlist") will navigate you to the full Property Details page.

# Chapter 4: Exploring Property Details

Once you find a property that interests you in the catalogue or search results, clicking on it will take you to its dedicated Property Details page. This page provides comprehensive information about the listing.

## 4.1 Viewing Property Information

The Property Details page is structured to give you all the necessary information at a glance and in-depth.

*   **Navigation:**
    *   A "Back to results" link is usually present to easily return to your previous search or catalogue view.
*   **Main Header Section:**
    *   **Property Title/Name:** The prominent name of the property.
    *   **Badges:** Similar to the property card, showing type (e.g., "House," "Land"), listing type ("For Sale," "For Rent"), and "Featured" status.
    *   **Full Address:** The complete address including locality, city, and pincode.
*   **Action Card (Sticky Sidebar):**
    *   On wider screens, an action card often appears in a sidebar and stays visible as you scroll.
    *   **Price:** Clearly displayed, with "per month" for rentals.
    *   **Advance Amount (for Rentals):** If applicable.
    *   **Primary Action Button:** This button changes based on the property type and your interaction status:
        *   "Book a Visit" / "Buy Visits to Book"
        *   "Apply to Rent" (for rentals, after a visit)
        *   "View Application Status" (if an application is active)
    *   **Wishlist Button:** Add or remove the property from your wishlist.
    *   **Share Button:** Opens a modal to share the property.
    *   **Info Text:** Provides context about the primary action (e.g., "Booking uses 1 visit credit").

## 4.2 Image Gallery

A prominent section displaying photos of the property.

*   **Main Display Image:** A large image is shown.
*   **Navigation Arrows:** If multiple images exist, left and right arrows allow you to cycle through them.
*   **Image Counter:** Shows the current image number and total images (e.g., "3 / 10").
*   **"View Gallery" / Lightbox:** Clicking the main image or a "View Gallery" button opens a lightbox modal.
    *   The lightbox displays images in a larger, focused view.
    *   You can navigate through images within the lightbox.
    *   A close button (<kbd>X</kbd>) allows you to exit the lightbox.
*   **Thumbnails:** Below the main image, a strip of smaller thumbnail images may be displayed. Clicking a thumbnail updates the main display image.

## 4.3 Key Features & Specifications

These sections break down the property's attributes:

*   **Key Features Section:**
    *   Highlights the most important attributes in a visually appealing grid.
    *   Examples: Area (e.g., 1200 sq.ft), Beds (e.g., 3), Baths (e.g., 2), Parking, Furnishing Status, Land Type, Building Type, Total Floors.
*   **Description Section:**
    *   A detailed textual description provided by the property owner/agent, highlighting unique selling points, neighborhood details, etc.
*   **Specifications Section:**
    *   A list of more detailed attributes.
    *   **For Houses:** Type of House (Apartment, Villa), Balconies, Total Floors (in building), Floor Number (if apartment), Facing Direction, Corner Plot status, Water Source, Power Backup, Lift Facility (for apartments).
    *   **For Land:** Type of Land, Plot Dimensions, Road Access Width.
    *   **For Buildings:** Type of Building, Total Units, Available Units, Common Amenities (listed).
*   **Nearby Amenities Section:**
    *   Lists distances to common facilities like hospitals, bus stops, schools, gyms, parks, and swimming pools.
    *   Distances are shown with the selected proximity unit (e.g., km, meters, minutes walk/drive).
*   **Video Tour Section (if available):**
    *   If a YouTube video URL is provided for the property, an embedded YouTube player will allow you to watch a video tour.

## 4.4 Location on Map

*   If the property has latitude and longitude coordinates provided, an interactive map will display its precise location.
*   A marker indicates the property's position.
*   You can zoom and pan the map.
*   Clicking the marker might show a small pop-up with the property name.

## 4.5 Sharing a Property

You can easily share a property with others:

1.  Click the **Share Icon** on the Property Details page.
2.  A **Share Sheet Modal** will appear.
3.  This modal provides options to share via:
    *   **Social Media Platforms:** WhatsApp, Facebook, X (Twitter), Instagram.
    *   **Copy Link:** Copies the direct URL of the property details page to your clipboard.
4.  Select your preferred sharing method.
    *   For direct social media shares, a new window or tab will open, pre-filled with a message and the property link.
    *   For "Copy Link," you'll receive a notification that the link has been copied.

# Chapter 5: Interacting with Properties

This chapter details how you can engage with properties you find on the platform, from saving them to your wishlist to booking visits and applying for rentals.

## 5.1 Managing Your Wishlist

The Wishlist allows you to save properties you are interested in for easy access and to track your interactions with them.

*   **Adding to Wishlist:**
    *   On any property card (in the catalogue or homepage) or on the Property Details page, look for the **Heart icon** (<kbd>❤️</kbd>).
    *   Clicking the Heart icon will add the property to your Wishlist. The icon will typically fill in or change color to indicate it's wishlisted.
    *   You need to be logged in to use the Wishlist feature. If you're not, you'll be prompted to log in.
*   **Viewing Your Wishlist:**
    *   Access your Wishlist by clicking the **Heart icon** (<kbd>❤️</kbd>) in the main navigation bar or by selecting "My Wishlist" from your user profile dropdown menu.
*   **Wishlist Page:**
    *   Properties in your wishlist are displayed as cards, similar to the catalogue.
    *   Items are typically grouped by their **Interaction Status** (e.g., "Visit Pending," "Wishlisted," "Visit Completed").
    *   Within each group, items might be sorted by relevance (e.g., upcoming visit dates first, most recently wishlisted first).
*   **Removing from Wishlist:**
    *   On a Wishlist item card, click the **Trash icon** (<kbd>🗑️</kbd>) or a filled Heart icon to remove the property from your wishlist.
    *   You can also un-wishlist a property from its main Property Details page by clicking the filled Heart icon.
*   **Interaction Status:**
    *   Each item in your wishlist will show its current interaction status (e.g., "Wishlisted," "Visit Pending," "Visit Completed," "Rental Application Submitted"). This helps you track your journey with each property.
    *   Some items might also display assigned agent contact details if a visit has been scheduled or an application is in progress.

## 5.2 Booking Property Visits

The platform allows you to request visits to properties you are interested in.

### 5.2.1 Understanding Visit Credits

*   To book property visits, you typically need **Visit Credits**.
*   Your current visit credit balance and its expiry date are usually displayed in the navigation bar when you are logged in.
*   Each visit request consumes one credit.

### 5.2.2 Purchasing Visit Plans

If you run out of visit credits or your plan expires, you can purchase more:

1.  Navigate to the **"Buy Visit Credits"** or **"Plans"** page, accessible from the main navigation bar.
2.  You'll see a list of available visit plans, each offering a certain number of visits at a specific price.
3.  **Select a Plan:** Click on the plan that best suits your needs. The selected plan will usually be highlighted.
4.  **Proceed to Payment:** Click the "Proceed to Payment" button.
5.  **Payment Gateway:** You will be redirected to a secure third-party payment gateway (e.g., Razorpay) to complete your purchase.
    *   Enter your payment details as required.
    *   Upon successful payment, you'll be redirected back to the platform.
6.  **Confirmation:** Your visit credit balance will be updated. You can also check your "My Transactions" page for a record of the purchase.

**Booking a Visit:**

1.  Find a property you wish to visit either from the **Property Catalogue** or your **Wishlist**.
2.  On the property card (detailed view) or the Property Details page, click the **"Book a Visit"** button (often with a <kbd>📅</kbd> icon).
    *   If you have no visit credits, this button might change to "Buy Visits to Book" and link to the Plans page.
3.  A **Visit Booking Modal** will appear:
    *   **Select a Date:** Choose your preferred visit date from the calendar. Dates are usually restricted to the near future (e.g., within the next 3 weeks, excluding today).
    *   The modal will display the property name you are booking for.
    *   An informational message will remind you that booking uses a visit credit and to check your wishlist for status updates.
4.  Click **"Request Visit"** or **"Confirm Booking"**.
5.  **Confirmation:**
    *   If successful, one visit credit will be deducted from your balance.
    *   The property's status in your Wishlist will change to "Visit Pending" or a similar status indicating the request has been made.

## A.3 Support Ticket Statuses

These statuses indicate the current stage of your support request or inquiry. You'll see these in the "My Support Tickets" section.

*   **NEW:** Your ticket has been successfully submitted and is awaiting review by the support team.
*   **OPEN:** A support team member has acknowledged your ticket and may be looking into it.
*   **ASSIGNED:** Your ticket has been assigned to a specific support agent or team member.
*   **WAITING_TENANT_RESPONSE:** The support team has replied and is awaiting further information or a response from you (the tenant/raiser).
*   **WAITING_OWNER_RESPONSE:** (More relevant for admin/landlord view) The support team is awaiting a response from the property owner.
*   **IN_PROGRESS:** The support team is actively working on resolving your issue or inquiry.
*   **RESOLVED:** The issue reported in your ticket has been addressed or your inquiry has been answered. The ticket might automatically close after a period if no further response is received.
*   **CLOSED:** The ticket is considered fully resolved and has been closed. No further action is typically taken on a closed ticket.
*   **CANCELLED:** The ticket was cancelled, either by you or by the support team (e.g., if it was a duplicate or submitted in error).
